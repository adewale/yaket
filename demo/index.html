Yaket in the browser

A simpler demo of what YAKE is actually good at.

Yaket is a TypeScript port of YAKE for Single-document keyword extraction. It works with No training corpus and relies on Local statistical features like frequency, position, spread, casing, and co-occurrence instead of embeddings or external corpora.

Why this page matters

  • It shows YAKE on samples where phrase extraction is more legible than the old short Kaggle snippet.
  • It explains how to read the ranking instead of assuming users already know the YAKE scoring convention.
  • It keeps the browser story visible without turning the demo into a product landing page.

What to look for

Lower score means more relevant. Check the top phrases, their occurrences, and their sentence spread to decide whether the ranking is surfacing stable concepts or short-lived fragments.

    Where Yaket fits

    Good for article tagging, search indexing, newsletter topic extraction, and lightweight chunk metadata. If you want semantic similarity instead of deterministic phrase extraction, compare it with embedding-heavy tools like KeyBERT.
